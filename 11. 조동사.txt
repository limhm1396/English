[조동사]
동사를 도와주는 역할
조동사 + 동사원형

[조동사의 역할]
1. 조동사 + 동사원형
He speaks English. -> He can speak English.
2. 의미 추가
3. 부정문 담당
He speaks English. -> He can't speak English.
4. 의문문 담당
He speaks English. -> Does he speak English?
*의문문으로 바꿀 때 주어와 동사의 자리가 바뀐다.
5. 인칭 담당
6. 시제 담당
He spoke English -> He could speak English.

[일반조동사]
의미를 가지지 않고 오직 동사를 보조하는 역할
be, do, have

[be]
1. 진행형
be V-ing: ~하는 중이다
2. 수동태
be + PP: ~되다, 지다, 받다

[do]
1. 의문문 혹은 부정문으로 바꿀 때 사용한다.
You study English. -> Do you study English? -> You don't study English.
2. 동사의 의미를 강조하는 부사처럼 사용한다.
I love you -> I do love you.
3. 일반동사를 대신하는 대동사로 사용한다.
I Like your book. -> You do(= like it)?

[have]
1. 완료시제 -> have PP

[의미조동사]
의미를 추가하고 보조하는 역할
can, could, may, might, must, ought to, shall, should, will, would 등

[can]
1. 의미
- 능력: ~할 수 있다
I can speak English.
You can improve your English!
- 허락: ~해도 좋다
Can I go to the bathroom?
Can I change the color?
- 가능성: ~할 가능성도 있다
Bad things can happen.

2. 부정형
You can't get in there.
You cannot get in there.
*can't을 일반적으로 쓰고 cannot은 심각하거나 강조할 때 쓴다.

3. cannot have PP: ~했을 리가 없다
이미 있었던 일, 소문에 대해서 그럴리가 없어 하고 추측하는 표현
She cannot have done such a thing.

[could]
1. 의미
- 과거의 능력(can의 과거형): ~할 수 있었다
I could play the drums when I was a high school student.
- 추측을 담아 가능성을 이야기할 때: 할 수 있을 것 같다.
I could finish my book soon if you help me.
Sorry, I couldn't go to the party tonigh.
- 정중하게 부탁할 때
Could you please come earlier and help me?
Could you drive me home please?

2. 부정형
I couldn't care less.
I could not agree more.

3. could have PP: ~할 수도 있었을 텐데
I could've been your girlfriend.

4. couldn't have PP: ~했을 리가 없다, ~할 수가 없었다
We couldn't have won the game without him.
I couldn't have said it better myself.
*긍정 조동사 + have PP는 후회가 담겨있고 부정 조동사 + have PP는 현실을 부정하는 추측인 경우가 많다.

[may]
can보다 조금 더 조심스럽게 부탁할 때 쓰는 표현
추측의 의미가 담겨있다.

1. 의미
- 허락: ~해도 될까요?
May I help you?
May I see your ID?
- 추측: 아마 ~일지도 모른다
He may be at home.
As you may know, I am pregnant.
- 기원문: ~하기를!
May God be with you!
May your days be merry and bright!

2. 부정형
You may not take photos inside the museum.
I may not always be right, but I'm never wrong.

3. may well: ~하는 것이 당연하다, ~라고 생각하는 것도 무리가 아니다
You may well say so.
They may well not cooperate with us.

4. may as well: ~하는 것이 더 낫다
You may as well give it to me.
I may as well go to bed now.
*실제로 추측을 표현할 때 may대신 might를 더 자주 쓴다.

[might]
1. 의미(약한 추측): ~할지도 모른다
It might be true.
She might be right.
I might be out of town.

2. 부정형
Writing a poem might not be the best way to ask her out.
This might not be the most appropriate job for you.

3. might have PP: ~했을지도 모른다
He might have had an accident.

[would]
공손하게 제안하거나 원하는 것을 말할 때 자주 쓴다.

1. 의미
- 과거에 본 미래(will의 과거형): ~할 예정이었다
She said she would come back before dark.
- 공손한 제안
Would you like to have something to eat?
Would you mind if I join you?
- 공손한 기호 표시
I would like to have some Chinese food.
I would love to spend time with you!
- 특정 상황을 가정하고 의견을 표현할 때: (~라면)~할, ~일 것 같다
I would do the same.
I would never say that.
I would say no.

2. 부정형
You wouldn't tell on me, would you?
Having a child would not be a good idea for us.

3. would rather A than B: B할 바에 차라리 A 하겠다
I would rather kill myself.
I would rather walk then take a bus.
I would rather be deceived than deceive other.

4. would have PP: ~했더라면 ~ 했을텐데
If she wanted to be a teacher, she would have made an excellent teacher.

[must]
1. 의미
- 강력한 의무: 반드시 ~해야 한다
The youth in Korea must serve in the military.
You must help each other.
- 강력한 추측: ~임에 틀림없다
He must be sick because he never skips his workout.
You must be joking.

2. 부정형
You must not lose this medal.
Beggars must not be choosers.

*must는 매우 강력한 느낌을 주기 때문에 조심해서 써야한다.

3. must have PP: ~했음에 틀림없다
It must have hurt.
She must have lied to you.

[have to]
1. 의미
- (강력한 의무) ~해야 한다
You have to brush your teeth morning and night.

2. 부정형
= ~할 필요가 없다
You don't have to do this if you don't want to.

*have to는 must만큼이나 강력한 표현이므로 아주 중요한 사안이 아닐 때 have to보다 should를 쓰는게 낫다.

[should]
가장 유연한 표현으로 일상에서 자주 쓴다.

1. 의무
- 의무: ~해야한다
Young people should be educated.
You shouldn't lie
- 강력한 제안: 꼭 ~해라
You should go to see a doctor.
He shoyld be a singer!
- 부정형
You shouldn't change your mind after adopting a dog.
You shouldn't ask those kind of questions.

2. should have PP: ~를 해야했는데(하지 않았다는 의미)
You should have seen his face!

3. shouldn't have PP: ~를 하지 말아야 했는데(했다는 의미)
I shouldn't have said that.
*should와 현재완료가 만나면 후회를 나타내는 표현이 된다.

[had better]
= ~해라. 큰코다치기 전에!

1. 의미
- 충고, 경고: ~ 하는 것이 더 낫다
You'd better hurry uo.
You'd better trust me.

2. 부정형: ~하지 않는 것이 더 낫다
you had better not be doing it.
There had better not be anything missing.

*had better는 충고보다 경고에 사용된다.
